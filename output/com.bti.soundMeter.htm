Professional sound level meter for your device.
It measures loudness or sound pressure (SPL) in decibels.

Features:
───────
■ direct reading in dB
■ 0 to 100dB+ (decibels) range
■ fast meter response
■ max/min values display
■ selectable falloff speed
■ 5% max error (device dependent)
■ A and C weighting method
■ predefined and custom calibration
■ vibrating alert on defined value
■ source: microphone or music played
■ classic vintage analog look
■ meter rotation
■ 2 meter faces
■ 18 case skins
■ free

Notes:
▔▔▔▔▔
1. Due to microphone limitations on different devices the meter range is different, usually the low end of the scale cannot be achieved because of the microphone noise and the upper end of the scale is limited by the microphone distortion on high levels.

2. Devices employing AGC (Automatic Gain Control) will have significant measurement errors and limited range. This is not app's fault.

3. If you have measuring problems or doesn't work at all, try to disable "Use high sampling rate" in settings, restart the device and recalibrate.

We had calibrated many android devices with the actual sound level meter with dB(A).

deciBel is the result of a serious effort to make an Android based SPL meter as close to a professional-grade device as the phone hardware limitations allow.

It uses a Fast Fourier Transform algorithm to implement a high precision A weighting filter and sophisticated digital signal processing techniques to achieve the best possible SPL readings out of your Android phone or tablet.

It displays the current, maximum and minimum values in decibels, and generates 3 kinds of sound pressure level versus time charts. The display can be turned upside down to allow pointing the mic at the sound source.

deciBel is supported by ads.

### Please calibrate before the first use! ###

- true A-Weighting (ANSI S1.4)
- simple calibration interface
- numeric dB, dBmin and dBmax display

- sliding max, min and average soud level versus time chart
- accumulated sound level exposition chart (dosimeter)
- saves chart screen shots to the SD card
- dominant frequency display
- 22Khz bandwidth at the input of the weighting filter
- 1024 point real time FFT and digital sound processing
- movable to the SD card
